# Metacognition Features Explained

## What is Metacognition?

**Metacognition = "Thinking about thinking"**

It's your ability to:
1. **Monitor** your own understanding ("Do I really get this?")
2. **Control** your learning ("What strategy should I use?")
3. **Evaluate** your progress ("Did that help?")

### 3. Confidence Slider (MONITOR Phase)

**"How confident are you in your answer?"**

Slider from 0% to 100%

**Why it matters:**
- Develops **calibration** - knowing when you really understand vs. when you're fooling yourself
- Compares your perceived confidence to actual performance
- Poor calibration is a sign of the "Dunning-Kruger effect" (overconfidence in beginners)
- Good learners know what they don't know

**Metacognitive skill:** Self-monitoring and calibration

## How to Maximize Metacognitive Benefits

### For Learners

**During PLAN:**
- Take 10 seconds to really think about your goal
- Choose a strategy BEFORE reading the content
- Notice which strategies you default to

**During MONITOR:**
- Don't rush the explanation
- Be honest with your confidence level
- Note what confuses you (muddiest point)

**During EVALUATE:**
- Compare your answer to the expected points carefully
- Really reflect: Did your strategy help?
- Commit to ONE concrete adjustment

### For the System (Future Enhancements)

**Track over time:**
- Strategy effectiveness by topic type
- Calibration trends (improving? overconfident?)
- Most common muddy points
